Dementia is a general term for loss of memory, language, problem-solving and other thinking abilities that are severe enough to interfere with daily life. Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia.

Dementia is not a single disease; it's an overall term that describes a group of symptoms associated with a decline in memory or other thinking skills severe enough to reduce a person's ability to perform everyday activities. Alzheimer's disease accounts for 60-80% of cases.

Early signs of dementia include difficulty remembering recent events, problems with planning or solving problems, confusion with time or place, trouble understanding visual images and spatial relationships, and problems with words in speaking or writing.
