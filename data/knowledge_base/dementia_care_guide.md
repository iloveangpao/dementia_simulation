# Dementia Care Knowledge Base

## Mild Dementia (Early Stage)

### Symptoms
- Occasional memory lapses, especially for recent events
- Difficulty finding words or names
- Trouble organizing and planning
- Mild confusion about time and place
- Generally maintains social skills

### Communication Tips
- Be patient and understanding
- Use simple, clear sentences
- Give one instruction at a time
- Allow extra time for responses
- Validate their feelings

### Care Strategies
- Maintain familiar routines
- Use memory aids like calendars and notes
- Encourage independence when safe
- Provide gentle reminders
- Keep social interactions positive

## Moderate Dementia (Middle Stage)

### Symptoms
- Significant short-term memory problems
- Confusion about people, places, and times
- May not recognize familiar faces
- Difficulty with daily activities
- Increased repetition and agitation

### Communication Tips
- Speak slowly and clearly
- Use validation therapy
- Avoid arguments or corrections
- Focus on feelings, not facts
- Redirect when agitated

### Care Strategies
- Establish consistent daily routines
- Use visual cues and reminders
- Simplify tasks and environment
- Provide supervision for safety
- Respond to emotions, not behaviors

## Severe Dementia (Late Stage)

### Symptoms
- Severe memory impairment
- Limited vocabulary and communication
- May not recognize family members
- Requires help with all daily activities
- May have physical symptoms

### Communication Tips
- Use very simple words and phrases
- Focus on tone of voice and touch
- Maintain eye contact
- Be very patient and gentle
- Use non-verbal communication

### Care Strategies
- Provide total care assistance
- Maintain comfort and dignity
- Use music and sensory stimulation
- Keep environment calm and safe
- Focus on quality of life

## General Dementia Care Principles

### Validation Therapy
- Acknowledge their reality
- Don't argue with their perceptions
- Validate their emotions
- Use empathy and understanding
- Enter their world

### Behavioral Management
- Look for triggers of agitation
- Redirect attention to positive activities
- Use calm, soothing voice
- Provide comfort and reassurance
- Maintain consistent caregivers

### Environmental Considerations
- Reduce noise and distractions
- Ensure good lighting
- Remove safety hazards
- Use familiar objects and photos
- Create calm, comfortable spaces

### Family and Caregiver Support
- Provide education about dementia
- Offer respite care opportunities
- Connect with support groups
- Encourage self-care
- Professional counseling when needed
